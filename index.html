Who She Was

Born to Reach
the Stars

Kalpana Chawla was an Indian-American astronaut and aerospace engineer who became a symbol of courage, intellect, and limitless ambition. Born in Karnal, Haryana in 1961, she dreamed of flight from childhood — sketching aircraft and watching planes at the local flying club.

She moved to the United States determined to turn her passion into purpose, earning degrees from three universities before joining NASA as a research scientist and later a mission specialist. Her two space shuttle flights made history — and her legacy still inspires millions of young women in STEM across the world.

Her Journey Through Time

From Karnal to the Cosmos

1961 — Born in Karnal, Haryana
A Dream Is Born ✨
On July 1, 1961, Kalpana Chawla was born in Karnal, India. From childhood she was fascinated by aircraft — she would visit the local flying club with her father, watching small planes take off and dreaming of the sky above.
1982 — Punjab Engineering College, Chandigarh
First in Her Class to Dare ✈️
Graduated with a B.Tech in Aeronautical Engineering — the only woman in her batch. Despite societal pressure, she chose the most challenging engineering discipline and excelled, driven purely by passion for flight.
1984 — University of Texas, Arlington
Crossing Oceans for the Sky 🌊
Kalpana moved to the United States for her Master of Science in Aerospace Engineering. Far from home, in a new country, she was relentless — her eyes always fixed on a destination beyond the atmosphere.
1988 — NASA Ames Research Center, California
PhD & Into the Labs of NASA 🔬
After completing her PhD from the University of Colorado Boulder, she joined NASA Ames to research computational fluid dynamics — simulating complex airflows around aircraft with powered-lift systems. She also earned her FAA commercial pilot's license during this period.
1994 — NASA Johnson Space Center, Houston
Selected as Astronaut Candidate 🚀
NASA chose Kalpana as part of Group 15 — one of only 19 selected from thousands of applicants. She spent 1995 in rigorous astronaut training: spacewalk operations in the Neutral Buoyancy Lab, T-38 jet flying, robotics arm systems, and wilderness survival.
November 19, 1997 — Mission STS-87
India Touches the Stars 🌌
Aboard Space Shuttle Columbia, Kalpana became the first Indian-born woman to reach space. She logged 376 hours over 16 days, operated the robotic arm, helped deploy the Spartan satellite, and orbited Earth 252 times — covering 6.5 million miles.
January 16 – February 1, 2003 — Mission STS-107
Her Final, Eternal Journey ✦
On her second mission, Kalpana and a crew of 7 conducted 80+ science experiments over 16 days. On February 1, 2003, Space Shuttle Columbia disintegrated during re-entry at 200,000 feet. India wept. The world mourned. But she had always belonged to the stars — and that is exactly where she returned.

Her Mark on the Universe

A Legacy Written in Starlight

🛸
Asteroid 51826 Kalpanachawla
An asteroid in the main belt between Mars and Jupiter was named in her honour by the International Astronomical Union.
🏔️
Mars Hills Named After Her
Several hills in the Columbia Hills region on Mars were named after the STS-107 crew — Kalpana Hill among them.
🏫
Schools Across India
Hundreds of schools, colleges, and universities across India bear her name, inspiring millions of girls to dream beyond the sky.
🛰️
Cygnus SS Kalpana Chawla
NASA's Northrop Grumman Cygnus resupply spacecraft was named SS Kalpana Chawla, carrying cargo to the ISS in her honor.
🌸
National Role Model
The Government of India recognized her as a national role model. Her story is part of school curricula across the country.
🏅
Congressional Space Medal
Posthumously awarded the Congressional Space Medal of Honor — the United States' highest award for an astronaut.
